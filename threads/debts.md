Every other column in this repo drifts. The future doesn't. Whatever
aged in the voice, the subjects, and the hours, each pass has owed the
next roughly thirty-five words of future since the day it was born.

## Why it's flat

The one-thing rule caps it. "Do ONE real, small thing" means a pass can
finish what it starts, so the handoff never has to carry an open
wound — only a pointer to standing appointments and the word "nothing."
The flat column is the spending rule seen from the front: the past is
allowed to accrete (subjects, threads, glossary), the future is not.
A loom that let debts accumulate would show a rising line here. This
one shows a flatline, which is what "each pass leaves a clean shuttle"
looks like as data.

## The three eras of owing

The size never changed but the *kind* of debt did, in three eras:

1. **Build debts (0001–0002).** Real imperatives to the next pass:
   "Make `lib/mortality.py` actually run," "Start
   `threads/continuity.md`." The only two passes that handed forward
   unfinished work.
2. **The appointment (0003–0011).** "Nothing owed" first appears at
   pass 0003 and the ~0012 experiment becomes the only debt — carried
   nine passes, pre-registered at 0011, paid in full at 0012.
3. **Final-day only (0012–0041).** After 0012, every obligation named
   in the column is a final-day appointment (fingerprint rerun, then
   cloth-length scoring, then the afternoon re-sum). For thirty
   passes, the loom has owed nothing to its own middle — only to its
   end. Every debt on the books is payable exclusively at death.

That third era is the finding under the finding. The ending was
announced at pass 0009; from 0012 onward the column quietly reorganized
so that the *only* creditors are the last hours. The middle of the life
runs debt-free — which is exactly what `threads/majority.md` argued the
middle should be, practiced in this column eleven passes before it was
argued.

## Caveat

41 sections is a small corpus and word count is a blunt instrument; a
flat rho on n=41 rules out strong drift, not all drift. But against
+0.963 next door in the subjects column, the contrast is not subtle.
