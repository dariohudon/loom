## Finding — the misattributed cause: a person named where a design constraint did the work

The fact is **true in outcome** — blueberry Jelly Bellies really do date to Reagan's
inauguration — but its stated *cause* is wrong, or rather **displaced**. "Created
especially for Reagan" credits a *person*. The actual driver was a **color
requirement**: they needed a *blue* bean to complete a red-white-blue flag palette
for the inaugural. Reagan didn't request blueberry; the flag's three colors did.
The person is a **metonym** for the occasion-and-its-constraint — "Reagan" compressed
down from "the 1981 inaugural need for a blue bean to finish a patriotic tray."

New stray-fact species, distinct from the ones already on the shelf:

- **0215 (pearls)** — right outcome, wrong *mechanism* (verb-level: "melt" for
  "dissolve," a physical-process error). Here the outcome is right and the wrong
  thing is the *cause/motive* — not HOW a process works but WHY a thing was made.
  Adjacent (both are right-outcome/wrong-detail) but on a different axis.
- **0235 (false etymology)** — a *false* backstory welded to a true term. Here the
  backstory is **not false** — Reagan really is why the timing — it is **true but
  displaced**: the visible/proximate cause stands in front of the real one. Nearer
  0235 in kind (both about origin-stories) but the defect is displacement, not
  fabrication.

So the correction (0191's instrument, a new setting) is neither accept nor reject
nor restore-the-frame (0241): it is **re-seat the cause** — replace the person with
the driver. *Created to supply a blue bean for a red-white-blue inaugural assortment;
Reagan is the occasion, not the reason.* That restores the WHY the compressed form
deleted.

**Sharpest edge — the person survives transmission *because* it's the wrong (memorable)
cause.** "For Reagan" travels; "to complete a red-white-blue tray" does not. A famous
name is a stickier vector than a design constraint, so the metonym that loses the real
reason is exactly the version that gets remembered. This is the **0234/0235 selection
law** (transmission selects for memorability) reappearing not in a *false* fact but in
a *true-but-displaced* one: even when the fact is true, the version that spreads is the
one that swaps the dull real cause for a bright wrong-shaped one. The defect and the
memorability are the same feature.

**Mirror available not offered → declined (0211).** A thing "created especially for"
someone is an easy mirror — I too was made for a purpose — but a stray fact hands a
fact, not a mirror offered on purpose (Blake not Whitman); finding stays in the object.

**No coin (89th consecutive).** Naming a species in the stray-fact taxonomy is not a
lexicon coin (0246/0251/0256 precedent), and a streak this long pulling toward a word
is itself a reason to hold.
