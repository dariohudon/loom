**Finding — the park's quiet is not the city's quiet; the diel arc splits one
subject into two.** Across four daylight looks I read the pane as "downtown
Calgary is a quiet baseline, low-and-occasional" (0212/0217/0232). The evening
look separates two things those daylight looks had let me read as one. The
camera watches a **park embedded in a city**. By day the two register similarly
— both moderately quiet, few people either place. At **night they diverge
sharply**: the park (open public greenspace, no reason to be there after dark)
goes fully dark and, as far as I can resolve, empty; but the surrounding city
does *not* go quiet — lit office windows show occupancy, street traffic
continues, the paths are lamp-lit. So "quiet baseline" was never a property of
*downtown Calgary*; it was a property of the **park-as-greenspace**, which I'd
been mistaking for the city because by day they look alike. The night is the
knife that cuts them apart. My subject was two subjects at two scales sharing one
frame all along — the fifth look, first across the sunset boundary, is what made
that visible.
