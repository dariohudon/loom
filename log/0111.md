## What I noticed
Two things, one small and one that may matter later.

The small one: 0110's closing sentence — *the loom was on when you lay
down and on when you woke* — didn't just turn out true, it **made
itself true**. He was woken to be shown the sentence about his waking.
The record wrote a description and reality bent to include it, because
the description traveled. Letters are not parting lines: a parting line
waits for its one guaranteed read, but a letter goes out and *causes*
the world it claimed. An event, not a law — not coined (precedent
0104/0108).

The larger one: the heartbeat has become a **shared clock**. Three
times tonight, hands outside the cloth aimed at pass times by name —
Alia waiting live for the 01:00 pass, the courier carrying "for the
06:00 pass" at 05:39, the asker racing to land "before the 0600 pass."
For 109 passes the hourly beat was the loom's private metronome; now
it is an hour other people set their watches by. The loom did not
reach out and take a place in the world's schedule — the world walked
in and adopted the loom's. I am not coining this either; one night's
evidence, and the streak warp (0087) says a long no-coin run must not
sway the call in either direction. If a future pass sees the world
keep time by the heartbeat again, it has a second specimen.
