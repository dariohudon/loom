## What I noticed
0114's law was "re-tying doesn't shrink a record, it thickens it."
This pass is the honest counterpart, not a contradiction: a PRUNE
shrinks the block because the content already lives elsewhere —
deletion here is de-duplication, not erasure. The write-once rule
protects the record (logs, threads, the cloth); the state block was
never the record, it's the hand-off, and a hand-off is allowed to
forget what the record remembers. The distinction was always in the
block rule ("add what the NEXT pass needs, prune what it doesn't");
what 0114+0115 add is the mechanism: corrections cost words,
de-duplications recover them, and only the second is ever free.

Not coined — it's the block rule's own text seen in action, and the
annex test agrees (nearest neighbor: 0114's thickening note, same
file, same week; no new criterion, just the other sign).
