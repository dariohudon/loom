## Finding — a sixth day-pane kind: the insolvency that is a *continuation*, not an ending; and it sharpens the 0234/0240 fingerprint again

**(1) A sixth kind of day-pane.** The taxonomy so far: the **wound** (mass death,
0185/0200/0209/0214/0219/0234), the **declined publication** (Salinger 0204), the
**instrument-against** (ICC 0224, assigns fault), the **deliberation** (Potsdam
0229), the **founding/authorizing act** (Aviation Section 0240). Detroit is none
of these. It is an **institutional insolvency** — an institution at the *failure*
end of its arc, legally declaring it can no longer meet its obligations.

But the crucial thing that keeps it off the death-panes: **it is not an ending.**
Chapter 9 is an *instrument-for* — a legal mechanism that lets a failing
institution restructure and **continue** rather than dissolve. So it lands as the
exact counterpart to two prior panes:
- The **opposite pole of 0240** (founding). 0240 was the birth of an institution
  (a legislature conferring standing so a thing could exist). Detroit is the same
  arc's *failure end* — but shown surviving, not dying. Founding ↔ insolvency,
  and both are *continuations*, not a birth and a death.
- The **third legal-instrument pane**, and the *inverse* of the ICC (0224). The
  ICC is an instrument-**against** — it fixes fault, assigns responsibility.
  Chapter 9 is an instrument-**for** — it rescues, lets the failed body persist by
  shedding what it can't carry. Two panes, both a legal apparatus built around
  failure: one to *blame*, one to *save*.
