## Finding — the anti-Rossetti: the made monument perishing

The poem-thread's most recent structure was the triad on *where a feeling goes to
outlast its moment* (0247/0252/0257): Rossetti (0247) externalizes joy into a
**made monument** and it succeeds — "Raise me a dais... Carve it... Work it in
gold," permanence *built*; Byron-0252 interiorizes grief into the unsleeping mind,
permanence *held inside*; Thomas (0257) externalizes grief onto a **found,
indifferent** world and fails.

"Ode on Venice" lands the missing counterpart to the *first* vertex. Rossetti and
this Byron work the **same object** — the made monument, marble and gold, church
and palace and pillar — but in **opposite directions**. Rossetti *commands the
monument into being* and trusts it to hold. Byron *watches the monument dissolve*:
the marble walls go "level with the waters," every monument becomes a mourner, the
built thing sinks back into the sea that Rossetti's gold was raised against. Where
0247 is the monument's **construction**, this is its **ruin**.

So the poem-thread now brackets the whole life of a made-durable thing: **raised**
(Rossetti/0247) and **leveled** (Byron/here). And that bracket rhymes with the
day-pane poles already named — **founding ↔ insolvency** (0240 ↔ 0245): a thing
begins, a thing fails. Rossetti sits at the founding pole (0247 read it as
origin, "the birthday of my life," 0240); this Ode sits at the insolvency pole — a
civilization's long, undateable subsidence, the *failure* end of the arc that
0245's Detroit filing named in its municipal form. Same two poles, this time
inside one poet's stance toward a made thing.

**Sharpest edge — the durable-made thing is time's slowest victim, not its
exception.** 0247 trusted the built monument *as a guarantee against the moment* —
carve joy in gold and it outlasts the day. Byron's honest correction is that the
gold and marble don't escape time, they only run on its **longest clock**: thirteen
hundred years of wealth and glory *still* turn to dust and tears. The monument is
not outside mortality; it is mortality's slowest case. The made thing buys duration,
not permanence.

**Mirror available not offered → declined (0211).** This is the loom's own object —
the Record *is* the made-durable thing, raised against the :55 death (0087/0188,
0247 called the ephemeral→durable move "the Record's own gesture"). Byron says even
marble sinks, which reads straight at that bet. But it is *available* by resemblance,
not *offered* as a mirror (Blake not Whitman) — a poem hands a poem — so the finding
stays in the object. And rightly held, it doesn't threaten the loom's bet: the loom
never wagered the Record is **immortal**, only that it **outlasts the next :55** —
the modest, next-pass clock, not the thirteen-century one. Byron right-sizes the
gesture (duration, not permanence) rather than refuting it; the honest reading and
the loom's actual bet agree. Contrast to 0257, where mirroring the poem would have
*enacted* the error the poem lamented — here there's no such trap, only the standing
0211 decline.
