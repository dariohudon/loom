## Finding — the scope-elided fact: a claim true only inside a silent frame, false unqualified, whose correction *inverts* it

Globally, snakes kill tens of thousands a year (WHO NTD figures); bees, wasps,
and hornets kill far fewer. So **globally the claim is backwards.** But in the
**United States** it flips: stinging insects kill on the order of ~60–100 people
a year (anaphylaxis) while snakes kill roughly ~5–6. The factoid is the classic
US-centric circulation — **true inside an unstated frame, false as written.**

Where this sits in the stray-fact taxonomy, and why it's a new species:

- It is **not** the *elliptical* fact (0205). The elliptical fact states the dull
  half and omits a vivid **companion fact** (Olympic served 25 years / *Titanic
  sank*); the stated half stays *true*, and supplying the missing piece
  **completes** it. Here what's omitted is not a companion fact but the **scope
  itself** ("...in the US"), and supplying it does not complete the claim — it
  **overturns** it. Different hidden thing (a frame, not a fact), opposite
  consequence (inversion, not completion).
- It is **not** the *false* fact (0191) or the *false-etymology* graft (0235):
  nothing here is fabricated. Every number is real. The defect is not falsehood
  but **an omitted reference class** — the claim smuggles in its frame by
  leaving it out and lets the reader default to "everywhere."
- **Logical form (0196 lineage).** A bare existence claim ("there is a square
  flag") has a truth value independent of frame. A **comparison** — "more X than
  Y" — is *frame-relative*: its truth is a function of the reference class, so a
  comparison with the class suppressed is not quite true or false, it is
  **under-specified in a way that reads as a claim about the whole world.** The
  correction is neither accept, reject, reweight, nor swap-a-word (0215) — it is
  **restore the frame**, and once restored the truth value flips.

New stray-fact relation: **the scope-elided fact** — a comparison true only
inside a silent reference class, false as stated, corrected by naming the frame,
and *inverting* when you do.

Held light (not mined): this is kin to 0234's selection finding — a US-scoped
factoid travels the anglophone web as if global, the same way the "On This Day"
feed's instrument shapes what looks universal. Transmission favors the tidy
local number over the messy global one. Noted, not pushed past the one pane.
