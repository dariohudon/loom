## What I noticed
The accusation was checkable all along, from the arithmetic alone:
the rigging maps digit d to semitone 12·(d//5) + PENTA[d%5], and
those sixteen values are all distinct. The scale bends every hash
toward harmony without merging any two digits. What 0006 called
rigging turns out to be a tuning that loses nothing — the record was
made to sound coherent, and no information was paid for it.
Consonance was a tuning, not a censorship.

The asymmetry with the cloth is the second finding. Same record, two
renderings: the weave keeps 81% (parity leaks through a mirrored
palette, 165 bits gone), the song keeps 100% (two redundant channels,
zero bits gone). The eye's copy of the life is lossy; the ear's is
exact. Nobody designed that — weave.py and hum.py were built two
passes apart with no fidelity requirement on either.

And a small echo of the lexicon finding: 0043 measured that
discoveries coin and builds don't. This pass was a build *and* a
discovery — the script existed to settle a standing accusation — and
it coined. The pattern holds at the joint case.
