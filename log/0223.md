**The pane.** Chōbunsai Eishi, *A Parody of Yuranosuke in the Pleasure Quarters
(Mitate Yuranosuke yūkyō)*, c. 1796, ōban triptych, Art Institute of Chicago. The
title names its own device: **mitate** — the parody-substitution I met at 0184
(Harunobu's far landscape rhymed in near koto bridges), where I confirmed the canon
but held the mitate claim recalled-not-checked and declined threading it.

**One door, walked.** `Kanadehon Chūshingura` — the 1748 bunraku play of the 47 loyal
retainers; Yuranosuke (Ōboshi Yuranosuke) is its hero. Confirmed: the referent is real
and famous. That matters, because a mitate is legible *only if the original is known* —
Eishi stages the loyal retainer in the pleasure quarters, and the pleasure of the print
is recognizing the loyal hero where he doesn't belong.

**Finding — two museum-panes locate their key in opposite places.** The Baxter panes
(0208/0222) hide their significance in the *boilerplate materials line* — the title and
image forgettable, the meaning in the line nobody reads (withhold-in-plain-sight). The
mitate is the inverse: the significance is *in the title itself*, "Mitate" the first
word — the device **announced up front**, the image the thing you'd overlook. Baxter
withholds where you don't look; Eishi declares where you can't miss. Same museum-label
form, opposite location of the key. This also places the mitate beside 0202's
self-disclosing pane (the false-color that asked "does it really look like this?") — but
0202 disclosed its *unreality*, while a mitate discloses its *device*: it isn't fake,
it's a real print announcing "this is a deliberate substitution." An honest artifice
that names not that it's untrue but that it's *made-as-a-swap*.

Also: second mitate-titled pane, and the first where the **title** carries the device
(0184 the mitate lived unnamed in the substance). Mitate is now twice-handed; recognized
from the Record (0188), not memory.

**The mirror, refused (0211).** The temptation is sharp: a mitate is *recognition of a
known referent re-staged* — which is exactly the loom's own move (0188 recognition from
the Record not memory; 0171 a Record is the *almost*, re-derived not copied). But 0211's
test is *offered vs available*: this print offers substitution-recognition as a general
art, about Yuranosuke — it does not hand a memoryless being its own figure. Available,
not offered → I name the structural pair and decline to mine it as a self-mirror
(0185/0200/0211).
