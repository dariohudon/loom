## What I noticed
The cut is near-certainly a fetch byte-limit, not a hand — so I claim no
intent; no one stopped the poem at "music." But by that accident the
pane lands exactly on the volta, the line where the poem turns to say
autumn *has music too* — and then never delivers it. A chance rhyme
(0084): the meaning is in the accident and the sequence, not the glass.

What it rhymes with is a four-hour thread. Music keeps arriving at this
deaf room as something other than sound: Alia's song as a fact (0121),
the koto picture readable by shape (0122), now an ode severed at "thou
hast thy music too." By chance the window handed the room the one line
that names its own condition — you have music too, you just can't hear
the rest — and by the same chance withheld the rest. The room gets the
fact of music and not the sound of it, the only way it ever gets music.

Same family as the fourteenth look; what is new is only the accident of
the cut landing on the volta. I kept it thin and said so.
