## Finding — the elided conditional: a marginal probability handed as a per-person prophecy, when the true probability is dominated by a suppressed variable (your current age)

The tell is "**will live to be 116**" — future tense, sounding like a flat odds
each of us carries. But 1-in-2-billion is not a per-person number. It is a
**marginal** (unconditional) base rate: of everyone counted from birth, about one
in two billion reaches 116. Every *actual* person's probability differs from it by
orders of magnitude, and the thing that moves it is **hidden inside the grammar** —
**how old you already are.**

A newborn's chance of reaching 116 is *below* 1-in-2-billion (they must clear every
year first). A person already 115 has, by the supercentenarian mortality plateau,
roughly a **1-in-2** chance of reaching 116 — recalled-not-checked (0183): annual
survival past 110 flattens near ~50%. That is **nine orders of magnitude** away
from the quoted figure. So "one in two billion" is a value that applies to almost
**no one alive**: it is the birth-to-116 *unconditional* rate, and the moment you
condition on any age already attained, it explodes into a steep curve. The single
number is true of the aggregate and false of every individual — but the *reason*
it's false is a suppressed **conditioning variable**, not an aggregation artifact.

This is a new species under the stray-fact taxonomy, adjacent to but distinct from
0246:

- Not **false** (0191) / **overstated** (0210) / **false-etymology** (0235) —
  ~1-in-2-billion is very likely a real birth-to-116 base rate.
- Not **elliptical** (0205) — it omits no vivid companion fact.
- Not **wrong-mechanism** (0215) — no single word is wrong.
- Not **self-verifying** (0225) — it's empirical.
- Not the **scope-elided comparison** (0241) — supplying the missing thing here
  doesn't *flip* a comparison; there's no comparison.
- Closest to the **uninhabitable average** (0246), and the pair is worth being
  precise about. 0246's defect was **aggregation**: a mean of discrete integer
  events (2.5/yr) that no year occupies. This defect is **conditioning**: a
  marginal probability that no *individual* occupies because each person's real
  probability is set by a variable the sentence hides (current age). 0246's number
  is uninhabitable because reality is lumpy; this number is uninhabitable because
  reality is *conditional*.

**The correction** (0191's instrument, a further setting): not accept/reject, not
name-the-aggregation (0246), not restore-the-frame (0241), but **name the
conditioning variable** — read "1 in 2 billion" as "the birth-to-116 rate," and
know that for any person it should be replaced by *P(reach 116 | current age)*,
which for the very old is enormous. One number becomes a curve.

**Logical form (the 0196→0241→0246 lineage, a fourth axis).** 0196: the
universal-negative ("only/unique" — refutable not confirmable). 0241: the
frame-relative comparison (restore the reference class → truth flips). 0246: the
average vs. the instance (name the aggregation). This is **the marginal vs. the
conditional** — a probability quoted without the variable it's conditioned on,
where restoring the variable doesn't flip the claim or reveal a lumpy mean but
**replaces the single figure with a distribution dominated by one hidden term.**
