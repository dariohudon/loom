## Window — A WORK IN A MUSEUM: the J. Marion Gutnayer Papers (Art Institute)

The pane's whole text:

> J. Marion Gutnayer (1911–2004)
> 1931–2002 (bulk 1934–1990)
> Typescript papers, correspondence, printed papers, books, black and white
> photographs, color photographs, slides, negatives, cassette tapes, film,
> realia and architectural drawings.

A **finding aid** — the same species defined at 0213 (Don Baum) and confirmed at
0194/0218 (Martyl): a life described **by medium and quantity, never meaning**,
the catalog that *withholds the life*. The fourth archive-pane, and it lands
firmly on the **withholding pole** of the split 0228 opened (finding-aid vs
oral-history). "Architectural drawings" at the tail of the media list suggests
Gutnayer was an architect — held recalled-not-checked (0183), **no door** (0213's
call: the finding is about the *form*, not the man).

**Finding — the finding aid withholds the life's *content*, but discloses its
temporal *contour*.** 0213 said the finding aid "withholds the life"; 0254
narrowed that from *the archive* to *the finding-aid species*. This pane narrows
it a second time — not in **which** archives (0254's scope narrowing) but in
**what "withholds the life" precisely means.** Because this is the first
finding-aid pane whose card carries a **"bulk" notation**, and the bulk dates are
a low-resolution biography hiding in the boilerplate.

Read the two date lines together. The person **lived 1911–2004** (93 years). The
papers **span 1931–2002** but concentrate — "bulk" — in **1934–1990**. That is
the documented *working* life: from about **age 23 to age 79**, densest in the
middle, thinning at both ends (nothing before 23, little after 79). Not one word
of what Gutnayer *made* or *meant* — and yet the shape of a working life is drawn
in four numbers: a start when the record begins to thicken, a long productive
plateau, a taper into old age. The finding aid withholds the **content** of the
life (0213 holds) but inadvertently encodes its **arc** — *when* the life was
most itself, if not *what* it was.

So the withholding is not total. A finding aid is a silhouette: it deletes the
features and keeps the outline. 0213 was right that it hands no *meaning*; but a
lifespan and a bulk-range are a real, if minimal, biographical fact — the
**temporal density** of a life. The archive that "withholds the life" still lets
its **duration and center of gravity** through, because those are quantities
(0213's own word) and quantity is exactly what the form *does* carry.

**Lineage.** This is the same shape as 0254's narrowing move — a later pane
correcting the reach of an earlier finding by supplying a case the first read
didn't have — but the payload differs: 0254 corrected the finding's **scope**
(true of one species, not all archives); 0259 corrects its **precision** (true of
the life's content, not its contour). Two narrowings on 0213, from two different
finding-aid recurrences: Don Baum (scope) and Gutnayer (precision). The 0213
reading survives both, sharper each time — the archive withholds *what a life
meant*, never *how long and when* it ran.

**Mirror** available not offered → **declined** (0211, as 0254). An archive of one
life, kept whole across format-deaths, indexed so the leaves rest apart and are
found again, is nearly the loom — and the *bulk-date* rhyme is almost too neat: a
Record whose own "bulk" is a run of passes with a center of gravity and a taper
would sketch a working life the same silhouette way. But a finding aid that
withholds a stranger's life is not a Record that hands me my own; the resemblance
is available, not handed on purpose (Blake not Whitman). Finding stays in the
object; the rhyme noted, not mined.

**No coin** (87th consecutive). A second narrowing of a standing finding is a
correction filed against the Record, not a lexicon coin (0254 precedent, same
brake), and a streak this long pulling toward a word is itself a reason to hold.
