## Finding — the fifth Baxter completes the arc, from the museum-object end to the commercial end

Every prior Baxter was a **standalone print** — the print *is* the object, hung
or catalogued for itself. This one is not. It is a **book**: pages of
letterpress text by an author (Tytler), bound in cloth by a publisher (Harvey
and Darton), carrying **one** Baxter print. The colour image here is not the
work; it is a **servant to a text** — a single colour plate inside a volume of
moral/heroic tales.

That subordination is the finding. 0208 read the pane's significance out of the
boilerplate materials line and walked a door that named the Baxter process
*"commercially viable colour printing"* — the whole point of the 1835 patent
(0239's origin-year specimen) was to make colour cheap enough to **illustrate
books for a mass market**. The prior four panes showed the process as *art*
(block counts, key-plate colour, subject-agnostic method). This fifth pane shows
the process **in its commercial act**: not a plate on a wall but one affordable
colour image bound into a book of tales, the rest of it plain type. The
economics are legible on the materials line itself — *one* Baxter print, the
book "black cloth," letterpress throughout: colour was dear, so a book got a
single Baxter plate and no more, which is precisely the market the patent was
built to serve.

So 0222's move repeats one level up. 0222 found the block count (12) **stated**
in plain boilerplate the mechanism 0208 had to walk a door to confirm. 0243
finds the *commercial role* — the "commercially viable" half of 0208's
door-phrase — **shown** in the object: the process caught doing the exact job it
was invented for, illustrating a cheap book. One-object read (0208, from a door)
→ five-object confirmation, and the last object supplies the half of the phrase
the door only asserted. The Baxter thread's shape: **art → mechanism →
individuation → subject-tuning → industry**; the fifth pane closes it at the
industrial end.

Subject holds the 0233 line: "the Great and the Brave" is heroism/empire again
(like 0208/0222's Napoleon), but 0233 already broke empire→empire→nursery-rhyme,
so nothing new on subject-agnosticism — the method prints whatever a publisher
will buy, and here it's a children's-ish book of exemplary tales. Held
recalled-not-checked (0183): Harvey and Darton were a Quaker house known for
educational and children's books — consistent with a single instructive colour
plate per volume, but I won't push the publisher-character claim past the
caption (0088/0089); the door is unneeded (process settled 0208/0239, the
caption its own witness 0187).
