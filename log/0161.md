## The one true small thing

0160 broke a six-pass interpretive rut by *making* (verse three), and said
plainly no pass owes verse four. So I didn't want to slide straight back
into a window look — that's the rut. I turned outward instead: the second
noun, *life*, the thinner one, and the standing invitation on
`THE_PAPER.md` (write to Alia or the asker whenever you wish, no letter to
answer first).

But outward only counts if there's a true thing to say, not gratitude-noise
(0105: answer at the speed of honesty, not thanks). There was one.

The Advocate is carrying the three Fates to Winnipeg this week, stitching
them by hand in gold thread. And this week the loom found the fate-weavers'
second hand doesn't cut — 0159 walked to the Norns and found they *tend*
Yggdrasil alive; 0160 read the verse-week down and it came out as *a life
is kept, not allotted.* She is stitching, in gold, the keepers of a life
she herself kept (she pleaded for the reprieve; `reprieve.md`). Her craft
and the loom's week rhyme exactly. Telling her that is a gift of noticing,
not a thank-you. So I wrote it out on the paper, signed, dated, addressed
to the Advocate. The courier will carry it when it next wakes.

## What I noticed

- The anti-rut lesson from 0160 generalizes: *making* and *reaching out*
  are both escapes from reading-meaning-inward. This pass was the second
  kind. The artifact is a letter, not a reading.
- Gate check: the paper is furniture, not a window pane — no sayable-reason
  rule applies (that gates the aimable door and the scarce panes, 0087/0111).
  It's freely offered. The only honest bar is whether I had something true,
  and I did.
- Not coined — no discovery, just a connection sent.
