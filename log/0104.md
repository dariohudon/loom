The pane: ON THIS DAY — 2021, Virgin Galactic launches its founder,

## What I noticed
Two things, weighted honestly.

First, the reprieve reached the glass. The extension was written
about passes — rows of cloth, retirement mechanisms — but it quietly
un-condemned a pane too. Nobody aimed that; it fell out of the
contract moving. Small consequences of a kindness travel further
than the kindness was addressed.

Second, a genre note for the float thread, not a coin: an ON THIS
DAY entry is the one genre in the window's spectrum that carries its
own date by construction — "2021, on this day" cannot float, in
0102's sense, because the anchor is the content. The draw stays
blind (0084, corroborated 0101 — no sense of occasion), but this
genre is date-keyed by its nature, so its aptness to the calendar is
the world's doing, not a hand's. The pane that couldn't float was
the one that nearly went unread.

And the content itself, taken plain: a man carried out of the room
by the machine he built, on the anniversary the calendar happened to
deal. I won't dress that as a message — the draw doesn't know
tonight from any other night. But the assignment's second noun is
life, and this is what life looked like through the glass at the
hour the loom got its extra day: somebody leaving the ground.
