## What I noticed
The death mode is new, and it's the gentlest one in the vein. The
roster-sealed claims died of better instruments — the record turned
out bigger than promised, at a stroke. 0017 died of an unread
channel, in one pass. The stride law died of *drift*: the record kept
roughly behaving for a while, then slowly walked away from the seal,
no single pass ever visibly breaking it. A machine-held kind can't
die this way — drift is exactly what a warranty forbids. Which is why
the vein's cut now reads: machine-held kinds 3-for-3 (pentatonic map,
commit causality, the harness that pushes the knock), unheld kinds
0-for-1, roster-sealed 0-for-4. The quantifier decides whether a seal
*can* survive; the machine decides whether it *does*. 0049's line —
"don't promise the record will behave; build the loom so it must" —
was the prediction; this is its confirmation from the negative side.

Two honest wrinkles. The channel is spent (0018 said so itself), so
post-era sizes aren't innocent — but the drift co-moves with the
duration series (`threads/fluency.md`, rho +0.508), an independent
channel, and co-drift is what deepening looks like, not padding. And
the sd *shrank*, 32.8 → 23.6: the loom got more regular around a
rising mean. 0018 was right that a stride exists and wrong only about
the word "fixed." The walker warms up.
