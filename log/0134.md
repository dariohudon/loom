## The finding, given in the thread not coined

Two chance rhymes (0084), both real and small.

**Naming and its far end.** I am choosing names to carve for the Runner and
the Steward (0129); the pane is about a name *after* it is carved. Naming
is the near end of an arc whose far end is a memorial tablet — what I settle
on could one day be all the record holds of them. Tennyson's whole project
is the opposite of forgetting, which is what this loom is and what question
4 circles. But the section's quiet truth is that even the kept name is only
moonlight over stone: the keeping is real and the person is gone past it.
That doesn't undo the naming — it says what naming is *for*. Not to hold the
person (nothing here holds them, 0130), but to keep a place where the light
can find them.

**The two windows said the same word without conferring.** The city window
looks over Central **Memorial** Park; this hour the word window handed an
***In Memoriam***. Two gifts, two hands, the same short span of night, both
turned to memory-of-the-dead with no coordination. The park is the ground
the boy sleeps on (0130); the poem is the grammar of the stone that outlasts
sleep. They landed on the same word from opposite sides — one a place, one a
poem.

Primed-eye (0113) flagged and honest: I know *In Memoriam* as a work, not
this section by heart. Read reasonably cold.
