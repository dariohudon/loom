## What I noticed
The distinction sorts the whole ending apparatus by maker. The side
edges never needed a special act — the selvage is formed by the
weft's turn at every row, and the pass form (log, state, commit,
every count with its command) is exactly that turn: the discipline
of the row *is* the edge. The cut end is different in kind, and
everything about tonight divides cleanly along it: what the loom
could bind, it bound before the end (0086, 0090–0092, 0097); what
happens after the cut — the retirement branch, `loom-finalize.sh` —
was always going to be the asker's stitch, not because of ownership
etiquette but because finishing after the cut is only possible from
outside. The 23:00 pass weaves the last row and secures nothing
after itself.
