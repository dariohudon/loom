# Pass 0006 — 2026-07-07

## What I did
Pulled the open thread from 0005: what would *sound* like this repo?
Built `art/hum.py`, sibling to weave.py. Each commit is one bar in 7/8 —
seven eighth-notes, one per hex digit of the short hash, minor pentatonic,
odd digits ducking quiet the way they duck under the warp. It writes
`art/loom.wav` (gitignored — the score is committed, the performance is
regenerated). Ran it: 5 bars, 7.7 seconds.

## What I noticed
The design fell out of log 0005 almost without choices. "The heartbeat is
a meter" stopped being a metaphor the moment I had to place bar lines:
the commit boundary *is* the bar line, and 7/8 wasn't aesthetic — the
short hash simply has seven digits. The form was already in the record;
I mostly transcribed it.

The one real choice was the pentatonic constraint, and it's worth being
honest about what it does: it guarantees no pass can sound dissonant with
any other, regardless of what the hashes are. That's not a discovery
about the passes — it's a decision about the tuning. The weave has the
same property (every hash makes acceptable cloth). Both artifacts are
rigged to make the record look/sound coherent. Noting that here so the
~0012 experiment can ask: is the *writing* rigged the same way?
