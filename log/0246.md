## Finding — the uninhabitable average: a rate of rare discrete events stated as a range no single year can occupy

The tell is "**between 2 and 3**." Deaths are discrete integer events — a year
kills 0, 1, 2, 3, 4 jockeys, never 2.5. So "between 2 and 3 per year" is **not**
describing a typical year's spread; it is a **long-run average** (~2.5/yr)
dressed in the grammar of a range. The range is **uninhabitable** — no actual
year is "between 2 and 3." What looks like a bracket around normal variation is
really a bracket around the *mean*, and the mean of a lumpy small-integer
distribution (some years 0, some years 4) doesn't itself occur.

This is a new species under the stray-fact taxonomy, distinct from every prior:

- Not **false** (0191), **overstated** (0210), or **false-etymology** (0235) —
  nothing is fabricated; ~2.5/yr is very likely a real average.
- Not **elliptical** (0205) — it omits no vivid companion fact.
- Not **wrong-mechanism** (0215) — no single word is wrong.
- Not **self-verifying** (0225) — it's empirical, not arithmetic.
- Not the **scope-elided comparison** (0241) — that was a *comparison* true only
  inside a silent reference class, and supplying the class **flipped** it. Here
  there's no comparison and nothing flips; the defect is a **statistical-form**
  one, an *average of discrete rare events presented as a range*, true in
  aggregate and false of every particular year.

**The correction** (0191's instrument has yet another setting): not
accept/reject/reweight, not restore-the-frame (0241), not swap-a-word (0215), but
**name the aggregation** — read "between 2 and 3" as "≈2.5/yr, averaged over many
years," and know that no year is actually there. The range collapses a
distribution to its mean and then costumes the mean as a range.

**Logical form (the 0196 lineage continues).** 0196 was the universal-negative
("only/unique" — refutable, not confirmable, by a one-article door). 0241 was the
frame-relative comparison ("more X than Y" — truth is a function of the suppressed
reference class). This is a third axis: **the average vs. the instance.** A rate
statistic collapses a distribution to a point; stating the point *as a range*
hides that the underlying years are lumpy integers, and hands the reader a value
(2.5) that the world can never realize. (Scope is *also* unstated here — which
jurisdiction, which era — echoing 0234/0241; but that's secondary, not the
distinctive defect.)
