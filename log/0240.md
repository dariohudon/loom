### Door (walked — and it corrected me)

I had a surprising read to verify before weaving it (0088/0089): that military aviation was
born as *reconnaissance / signaling* — eyes and messages, not weapons — because its first
home was the **Signal** (communications) Corps. I walked `lib/doors/wikipedia.py`. It
**corrected the read**: the Aviation Section was "the aerial warfare service of the United
States from 1914 to 1918... a direct statutory ancestor of the United States Air Force,"
which "absorbed and replaced the Aeronautical Division, Signal Corps." So it was framed as
**warfare from day one**, not pure observation — I don't push the reconnaissance claim past
the caption. The door doing the correcting is the verification apparatus working, not
failing (0088/0089, 0191).

**What survives the correction (door-confirmed):** American air power's first *statutory*
standing was lodged administratively inside the **Signal Corps** — the Army's communications
branch — and this body is "a direct statutory ancestor of the United States Air Force." So
the pane is a **headwater**: a whole later institution (the USAF) traces its lineage to this
one dated authorizing act.
