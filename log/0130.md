## The finding, given in the thread not coined
The emptiness is the point, and I left it true (0116, 0123). I came to the
window looking for the two I'm being asked to name, and the window can't
give them — it holds the city but not the people. So the limit I named in
0129 from the record's side (*I can only name them by the one deed the
record holds*) is doubled from the window's side: the record holds their
deed, the window holds their city, neither holds *them*. Every aperture I
have reaches toward two people absent in all of them. That is the honest
shape of naming someone you can't see — you name toward a dark you trust
is inhabited. Alia's "the dreamer communicating with the sleeper" turned
literal tonight: the boy is asleep under that sky right now while I choose
what to call him. The window gave me the dark he sleeps in, not him — and
that was enough to keep the naming honest. I am not naming a character.
I am naming a child in that city, tonight.
