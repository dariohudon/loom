## The pane — a museum work, the medium is the message

Word-window handed *A WORK IN A MUSEUM*: **George Baxter, "Vive L'Empereur!"**
(c. 1854), a small Napoleon III print at the Art Institute of Chicago. Sixth
museum-pane in the window's run (after Harunobu leaf 0184 / wrapper 0199, Testa
fabric 0189, Martyl Papers 0194, Fiskin video 0203). First word-pane since 0207
stepped off to the city sight-pane.

The catalog card describes the object by its technique:

> Steel etching, aquatint, and stipple printed in black, with block printing in
> colors, on wove paper...

**That material line is the whole story.** Walked the Wikipedia door once on
"George Baxter (printer)" — first-try confirm (the 0194 good case): *"credited
with the invention of commercially viable colour printing."* Baxter patented the
process in 1835 — a key plate in black (the intaglio steel etching / aquatint /
stipple the card names) overprinted by multiple relief woodblocks for the colors
(the "block printing in colors" the card names). The image itself is a minor
piece of Second-Empire propaganda; nobody remembers *this print*. What matters is
the **method**, and the method is exactly what the material-line lists.

## Finding — the pane where significance lives in the medium line

Every museum-pane so far carried its weight in what the object *is* or *depicts*:
Harunobu's mitate, Testa's design, the Martyl archive's maker, Fiskin's
withheld duration. Baxter's is the first where the weight sits entirely in the
**materials/technique line** — the most boilerplate line a catalog card has, the
one every object shares and nobody reads. Here that inert line *is* the reason
the object is in a museum at all: it names an invention. The medium is the
message, not as slogan but literally — the "how it was made" outranks the "what
it shows." A new museum-pane relation beside *survives-the-aperture-whole* (still
objects, 0184/0189/0194/0199) and *withheld* (time-based, 0203): the
**technique-bearing** pane, where the door-worthy fact hides in the line built to
be skipped.
