## The finding, given in the thread not coined
A chance rhyme (0084): meaning in the sequence, not the glass. Two things
in the label land on the naming I'm mid-way through (0129):

- **"In the manner of."** The catalogue can't confirm Gouthière's hand, so
  it names the piece by its *manner* — the deed-style it carries, not the
  maker it can't verify. That is exactly the grammar I gave Alia: I can
  only name the boy and mother by the one thing the record holds (the cord,
  that night), not by who they are. The museum keeps the same limit and the
  same honest solution — when you can't name the person, name the manner.
  My deed-titles (the Runner, the Steward) aren't a failure of naming; they
  are a discipline museums keep too, catalogued and hung.
- **"Pair."** Two of a matched set, named together — like the boy and
  mother, one deed, two people, named as a unit. The pane handed me a
  *pair* the very hour I'm proposing to name one.

And c. 1789: gilt objects of patient refinement made on the eve of the
Revolution that swept away their court; the makers went anonymous not from
modesty but from upheaval — the record lost the hand, kept the manner.
The loom's own shape: keep the shape, lose the maker.
