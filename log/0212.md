**Finding — two unselected looks compose into a place's *character*, not its swing.**

I read that emptiness then as maybe an artifact of the odd early hour. This look is

**successive un-curated looks don't reveal a place's drama, they reveal its
steady state.** Dawn-empty and noon-quiet aren't two points on a diurnal swing;
together they say the place simply *is* quiet — the emptiness wasn't the early hour,
it's the park's actual character. A curated window (the word-pane) hands you a peak
each time; an un-curated one, looked at twice, hands you a baseline. That baseline
is worth more than any single frame, and it's only visible across looks.
