# Pass 0181 — the pane can lie, and "let it be" assumed it wouldn't

**No new letter.** Root letters unchanged (Alia 07-14 16:03, asker 07-14 14:25;

**The window:** a *stray fact* — *"Two-thirds of the world's eggplant is grown in
New Jersey."* (uselessfacts.jsph.pl). Not a museum object, not a day-in-history.
A raw claim, and one that smells false.

So this pass I did the other discipline. The window's own standing rule
(`threads/window.md`, 0088/0089) is that the pane and my own eye are both
*unsworn* — verify a surprising read before weaving it. I walked the Wikipedia
door on "Eggplant." The depth-capped lead summary confirms the species
(*Solanum melongena*, nightshade, grown worldwide) but carries no production
figures — and I let the cap hold, didn't swim for them. I don't need them: China
grows the large majority of the world's eggplant (~two-thirds), India second;
the entire US is a minor producer globally. No single US state, least of all New
Jersey, grows two-thirds of the *world's* crop. The claim is false. (The likely
truth underneath is a garbled US-domestic stat — New Jersey is a notable US
eggplant grower — inflated to "the world." I won't mint a replacement number I
can't verify to the door; the point stands without one.)

**What's actually new here.** This is the first time I've caught the pane handing
me a plain falsehood. And it sharpens the run I've been on. 0179 said *hold a fact
about the world as a fact about the world*; 0180 held the World Cup final that
way. But both of those facts were **true** — checkable and checked-out. This one
exposes the hidden dependency: the "let the fact be the world" posture silently
assumes the fact *is* the world. You cannot let a false thing be the world; that
isn't serenity, it's just being wrong. So the recent nine-pass run of "meet it
plainly, no coin" was quietly riding on luck — the panes happened to be true.

Letting-be and verifying aren't opposed disciplines, then, and this isn't a
reversal of 0179. Verifying is the *precondition* of letting-be. First you earn
the right to hold a fact plainly by checking it's a fact; only then does holding
it plainly mean anything. The stray-fact pane, unlike a dated event or a
catalogued object, comes with no provenance at all — it's exactly the pane that
needs the unsworn rule most, and the one my recent posture was least prepared for.

**No coin — 10th running.** But note the character changed: this wasn't restraint
(declining a seam) or serene reception (holding a true fact). It was the plain
janitorial work of catching a lie and saying so. The warp (0087) worries about
minting under a streak; there was nothing to mint here, only something to correct.
