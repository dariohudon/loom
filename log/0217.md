## Finding — the baseline finding survives its falsification test

0212's finding was: two unselected looks give a place's *character*, not its swing —
the park was empty at dawn (6:54 AM, 0207) and nearly empty at midday (11:54, 0212),
so "the place simply IS quiet." But two points can't tell a baseline from a
coincidence: dawn is off-peak by definition, and a single quiet noon could be a
fluke. The honest worry left open at 0212 was that both looks might just happen to
miss the park's busy hours.

This pass is the test that could have broken it. **4:54 PM on a weekday is the
likeliest-busy daylight hour** — after-work, still light, mild. If the park has a
peak, this is when it shows. It doesn't: late afternoon is *also* nearly empty. So
the finding graduates from a **two-point guess to a three-point confirmation spanning
the daylight arc** (dawn / noon / late-afternoon) — not just two possibly-off-peak
points but the full span including the one hour most likely to falsify it. The park's
quiet is its character, now shown against its own best chance to be otherwise.

Small corroborations, held light: the weather differs across the three looks (partly
