## Finding — the stale superlative: a ranking frozen at a receding peak

Two indices are missing at once, and the claim needs both to be true:

1. **Scope.** True within the EU (highest), nearly within the OECD (2nd) — false
   for "the world." This is the 0241 axis (an elided reference class), but the
   correction here does **not flip** the claim the way bees-vs-snakes flipped;
   it **demotes** it. "Highest in the world" → "highest in the EU, 2nd in the
   OECD." A shrunken frame, not an inverted one. So it's nearer **0210
   (overstatement)** than 0241: "world" overstates "EU."

2. **Time.** The rate "peaked in 1995" and has "constantly decreased" since. The
   claim is stated **tenselessly** about a quantity that is *moving* — and moving
   *downward*. This is the part no prior stray-fact species carried. Every prior
   defect was in a **fixed** attribute (a flag's shape 0196, a coinage 0210, an
   etymology 0235, an average 0246, a base rate 0251). A suicide rate is a **live
   statistic** that reorders yearly; a tenseless superlative about a live
   statistic is defective the moment the data updates, *independent* of any
   single error of scope. It is the first stray fact whose subject is a **moving
   quantity presented as a fixed rank.**

**Logical form (0196 lineage, a fifth point).** A superlative *is* a universal
negative — "highest in the world" = "no country is higher" — refutable by one
counterexample (0196's square-flag shape). But 0196's universal was **time-
stable** (flags don't change shape under you); this one **drifts**, because the
ranked quantity is alive. Lineage so far: universal-negative (0196) → frame-
relative comparison that flips (0241) → average-vs-instance (0246) → marginal-vs-
conditional (0251) → **superlative-over-a-moving-quantity (0256)**: a universal
claim whose truth is not fixed but *scheduled to expire*, because the world it
ranks keeps reordering.

**The sharpest edge.** The stale superlative doesn't merely misrank — it
**freezes a receding peak and hides the direction of motion.** The one hopeful
thing in the real data is that the rate has been *falling for thirty years*; the
tenseless claim erases exactly that, keeping Lithuania pinned at its 1995 worst.
A superlative about a moving quantity buries the *sign of the derivative* — and
here the buried sign is the good news.

**Correction (0191's instrument, a new setting).** Not accept/reject, not
restore-the-frame-and-flip (0241), not name-the-aggregation (0246), not name-
the-conditioning-variable (0251) — but **date-and-scope the superlative**: "as of
2021, the highest in the EU and 2nd in the OECD, and falling steadily since a
1995 peak." Two indices restored: *when*, and *among whom* — and with them, the
recovery the bare fact deleted.
