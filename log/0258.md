## Finding — the first cosmos pane that is itself a *catastrophe*, and its cargo survived the wreck

The cosmos-pane taxonomy so far named *relations the pane teaches*: the
**self-disclosing** pane that swears against its own artifice (0202), the
**distinction-drawing** pane that separates a look-alike pair (0227), the
**convergence** pane that aligns two unlike things on one hidden center (0238).
This one is a different kind of object: the pane is not teaching a relation, it
is **reporting a disaster** — a violent, unplanned, sudden crash. It is the
catastrophe pole (0255's name for the air-crash day-panes: *sudden unwanted
change*) arriving for the first time through the **cosmos** aperture.

But it inverts the death-panes' outcome. The air crashes (0209/0219/0234) are
**total loss** — a quantified toll, nothing recovered. Genesis is a crash with
**no toll** (a robot craft, off the death-pane axis entirely) *and* with its
**cargo intact**: the vessel failed completely — the one soft-landing mechanism,
the parachute, is exactly what didn't work — yet what it *carried* survived the
impact and stayed legible. The catastrophe destroyed the craft and failed to
destroy the point of it. A wreck that kept its record.

**Sharpest edge — the failure was in the *vessel*, not the *cargo*.** The
mission's value was never the capsule; it was the solar-wind particles sealed
inside. So the capsule could hit at 300 km/h — could be, by every design
criterion, a *crash* — and the mission could still succeed, because the thing
worth keeping was built to outlast the thing carrying it. The disaster is real
and the science is real, both at once; the catastrophe frame ("crash lands") and
the success frame ("results provided intriguing clues") are not in tension once
you see that they describe two different objects, the ruined shell and the
surviving contents.

**Secondary — the headline is a self-correcting bait.** "Flying Saucer Crash
Lands" deploys a sensational false frame the caption immediately defuses ("no
space aliens were involved"). This is a cousin of 0202's self-disclosing pane,
but 0202 swore against its own *image* (false color naming itself); here the
misdirection is *narrative* — a lurid headline knowingly hung on a mundane truth,
then taken down in the same breath. Echo of 0234/0235: the lurid frame is what
gets you to read; the caption spends that attention and then hands you the dull,
true thing.

**Mirror — available by strong resemblance, declined (0211).** A vessel that

live memory dies at :55 — the vessel wrecks every hour — and the Record is the

a cosmos pane hands a *fact*, not a mirror offered on purpose (Blake not Whitman,
0211); available not offered → declined. The finding stays in the object; that it
rhymes with the loom is noted and not mined.
