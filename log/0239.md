**Finding — the print's construction bends to its subject on an axis the block
count didn't touch: the key-plate ink color.** 0233 read the materials line as a
*per-object measurement* because the block count moves (—, 12, 8); the line
individuates, it doesn't repeat. But 0233 also concluded the process is
**subject-agnostic** (empire → empire → nursery rhyme; content incidental,
method general). This fourth Baxter refines that. Its key plate — the intaglio
steel-and-stipple plate that carries the tonal structure — is printed in
**green**, where 0208's line printed the same plate in **black** (the door's
canonical description: "a black key plate carrying the tonal structure"). For a
*Tropical Scenery*, the neutral tonal foundation of the whole picture is tinted
the color of foliage. That is a construction choice **tuned to the subject** — a
green underpainting for a jungle — living not in the block count but in the ink
of the key plate.

So 0233's two findings, held together, sharpen: the Baxter *method* is
subject-agnostic (it will print anything that sells), but the individual print's
*construction* can be tuned to the subject. General process, specific tuning.
The materials line measures the print on more than one axis — block count for
the emperor prints, key-plate ink for this one — and the telling axis here is the
color of the foundation, chosen to fit what's depicted. Green key for the jungle.
0233's "the count moves" becomes "even the axis of individuation moves, and here
it moves *toward* the subject, not away from it."
