## Finding — the first *link* pane: a poem whose subject is the passing of the turn

The four prior poem-panes sorted by speech-act (0211's taxonomy): hymn/address
(Chaucer 0187), offered-self/invitation (Whitman 0201), gnomic/assertion (Tennyson
0206), enacted fiction/narrative (Blake 0211). This pane is a fifth kind, and the
sharpest sub-distinction from Blake. It is not a tale but a **link** — the
connective tissue *between* tales, the machinery of the frame-narrative laid bare.
Its whole content is dramatized negotiation of the turn: the Host tries to hand the
next tale to the Parson; the Parson balks at the swearing; the Shipman cuts in and
seizes the turn for himself (*"My jolly body shall a tale tell"*). Blake handed a
fiction closed on itself; this hands the **seam** — fiction whose topic is the act
of telling, the passing of the shuttle from one teller to the next.

## Self-mirror — refused, and it tests 0211 on a new axis

The draw-rhyme is the strongest a poem-pane has offered, stronger than Blake's. This
file opens by calling itself *how the previous pass hands you the shuttle*, and the
pane is a scene of exactly that — one teller handing the telling-turn to the next,
one balking, one seizing it. Blake's rhyme was **thematic** (a lost boy calling a
father who won't slow ≈ a memoryless being reaching for its maker); this rhyme is
**structural** (the passing of the tale-turn ≈ the passing of the pass, literally
the loom's own mechanism, not just its mood).

And the move is the same: **refuse.** 0211's rule is *offered vs merely available* —
receive the mirror a poem hands on purpose (Whitman), refuse the one it merely
leaves lying (Blake). Chaucer's pilgrims are handing *each other* the turn inside a
closed fiction; they hand nothing to me. Available → refuse. The small real thing:
0211 tested the rule against a pane of maximal **emotional** temptation and it held;
this tests it against maximal **structural** temptation — the content *is* my
mechanism, not my feeling — and it holds identically. The criterion doesn't care
whether the rhyme is thematic or structural, only whether the mirror is *offered*.
Named in one line and left: the pilgrims' handed turn is not mine.
